TPC Signal Formation

This follows the detector story in order: two incoming particles collide, charged particles fly through the cylindrical gas volume, electrons are freed, and the endplate records ADC over time.

1
Two incoming particlesThe blue and red dots move toward the center of the TPC cylinder.
2
CollisionThey collide near the center and create new particles.
3
Charged tracksThe red, green, and purple curves are charged particles flying through the gas.
4
IonizationA close-up shows gas atoms and electrons knocked free near a track.
5
Drift to endplateFreed electrons drift along the electric field toward the readout side.
6
Recorded as ADCPads receive charge, and electronics store ADC values across time bins.
About the colored lines: they are not detector wires. They represent outgoing charged particle paths after the collision.
About time: `tbin` is readout arrival time. Electrons created farther from the endplate arrive later.
